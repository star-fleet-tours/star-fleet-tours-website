SpaceX NASA Crew-2 Mission

We're back with you all from COVID quarantine for the Falcon 9/Crew Dragon NASA Crew-2 launch to the International Space Station! The mission has been DELAYED one day and is now scheduled for No Earlier Than Friday, April 23 at 09:49 UTC (5:49 AM EDT) from historic launch pad LC-39A at NASA's Kennedy Space Center. This milestone launch will feature the first human spaceflight in a re-used capsule and liquid booster, and launch just before dawn, looks to feature a spectacular twilight effect if clouds cooperate.

We'd like to dedicate this mission to our longtime supporter and spaceflight community stalwart Wayward Plane, and will be donating all proceeds from our special NASA cookies, $10 from every pre-launch tour and any additional tip/donation you wish to give to Wayward's Mission to Beat Cancer. Wayward was one of the very first members of the spaceflight community to back Star Fleet, and has been an invaluable supporter, volunteer and friend to us ever since. We hope you'll join us in giving back to someone who's given their all to help hundreds of launch viewers and space enthusiasts through Twitter, Launch Rats and much more. #Wayward4Life!

STATUS UPDATE: Due to weather conditions downrange, the launch has been DELAYED one day to 5:49 AM EDT on Friday, April 23. Everything looks excellent for launch on Friday, we were able to rebook the boats and your tickets are still fully valid for that day as per our policy, but the times are now 20-30 minutes earlier to account for the new launch time, so please check below for that. Tours are still on for tonight as scheduled, as the rocket and capsule remains vertical on the pad, and the weather will still be great here. Importantly, however, due to the expansion from one boat and 15 people to almost a dozen boats and 60 people by popular demand, we have moved the tour checkin times 30 minutes earlier as well to ensure everyone has enough time to get on board; see the updated schedule below.

If you aren't able to go out with us, you can have your tickets transferred to a pre-launch tour (if you haven't already purchased one) so you can still see the rocket and capsule up close on the launch pad; we are happy to give you a full refund of your tickets. Either way, please let us know no later than 12 noon EDT Thursday, April 22 so we can ensure you're taken care of.

If you have any questions, we will have our email and social media online again to answer them, so feel free to ask. Make sure to stay tuned to our Slack, email list and social media for the latest important updates and to get in touch with us directly (see below for links). Thanks!

COVID-19 Safety Information

Safety is our number one concern, and no launch is worth risking people's health over. Therefore, in addition to all Star Fleet staff being vaccinated at least two weeks prior to the trip, we're implementing multiple procedures to ensure we all stay as safe as possible, including the following:

  • Limiting the number of passengers on our vessels to ensure we all can follow social distancing requirements (1.5-2 m between parties) while having an enjoyable trip.
  • Ensuring social distancing and minimal contact during checkin and on the boat
  • Requiring all Star Fleet volunteers and passengers to wear a face covering for the duration of the experience
  • Washing our hands and common surfaces prior to use
  • Providing hand sanitizer free for all passengers, at checkin and on the boat
  • Asking passengers who may be exhibiting symptoms, are at-risk for COVID or have a positive test to enjoy this launch from the comfort of their homes, or another safe location

If you have any questions or concerns, please do not hesitate to reach out and ask! We hope you are able to join us on a trip soon, and until then, we hope you stay home, stay safe, and enjoy plenty of space!
