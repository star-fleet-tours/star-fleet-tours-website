NASA SLS Artemis II Launch

Star Fleet plans to go out for launch viewing boat trips of the NASA Artemis II mission, the first time humans will go beyond Earth's orbit since the Apollo program. Currently, NASA is targeting the February launch window for the launch of the Space Launch System and Orion crew capsule, which lasts from Feb 6-11 (no launch on the 9th), with a final launch date to be confirmed following the Wet Dress Rehearsal. Therefore, launch will occur No Earlier Than Friday, February 6 at 9:41 PM EST (02:41 UTC February 7); with the launch time moving roughly 45 minutes later each succeeding day. We expect to be at a viewing location just outside the launch hazard area, with a clear view of the pad approximately 10 mi / 16 km away or closer (hazard zone dependent), with a much more favorable angle to the easterly trajectory relative to land-based locations.

We also hope to offer sunset boat trips prior to the launch, pending coordination with the relevant authorities, to see the Space Launch System rocket on LC-39B as it prepares for its historic mission. Join us to see the world's most powerful operational rocket as it stands tall on its launch pad for its very first crewed launch!

LATEST LAUNCH UPDATE: As of Tuesday January 27, final checks of the Space Launch System rocket at the pad appear to be going smoothly and the final key test before launch, the Wet Dress Rehearsal, has been moved forward to Saturday January 31. Immediately following this test, NASA will decide on and announce the mission's confirmed launch date, and we will open up tickets as soon as that occurs. As always, keep an eye here, on our social media and our WhatsApp community for the latest insider updates and official announcements as we get closer to launch!
